WHO WE ARE

HERIZON is committed to supporting the efforts of grassroots women, advocates, civil society organizations, educators, and youth leaders who strive to promote women’s empowerment and gender equality. It aims to build a peaceful, inclusive, and progressive society where women are respected, valued, and given equal opportunities to lead and thrive. HERIZON upholds the genuine principles of collaboration, integrity, and social responsibility in all its initiatives. It is an independent organization, neither politically aligned nor under the authority of any political or corporate entity.

*Melchora Aquino – Ang Ina ng Rebolusyon
Kilala bilang “Tandang Sora,” gumanap ng mahalagang papel si Melchora Aquino sa Rebolusyong Pilipino laban sa pamamahala ng mga Espanyol. Sa edad na marami ang umatras sa tahimik na pagreretiro, sa halip ay inialay niya ang sarili sa layunin, na nagbibigay ng pagkain, tulong medikal, at tirahan sa mga sugatang rebolusyonaryo. Ang kanyang tahanan ay naging kanlungan ng mga sundalong lumalaban para sa kalayaan, na sumasalamin sa diwa ng isang tunay na ina—hindi lamang sa kanyang mga anak kundi sa isang buong bansa.
*Inspiring Story of Robyn Mijares: Empowering Women & Youth Through Agriculture
Robyn Camilla Mijares is a young Filipina determined to bridge the gap between opportunity and potential. As the Founder and Executive Director of Grow School Philippines and Youth Uprising, she has turned her passion for sustainability into tangible programs that empower women, youth, and entire communities.
*Marilyn Martirez — From Struggles to Success
When Marilyn lost her husband in 2014, she suddenly became the sole provider for her five children. With little savings and great responsibility, she could have given up — but she chose courage instead. Through the government’s Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps), she ensured her children stayed in school and participated actively in community work.
*EDNA — The Solo Parent Who Never Gave Up
Raising eight children alone was never easy for Edna, but her willpower turned challenges into opportunities. Despite limited income, she found ways to provide for her family, often working long hours and helping neighbors in need. Through the DSWD Sustainable Livelihood Program, Edna received financial capital that she used to start a small sari-sari store. Over time, her small business grew, supporting her children’s education and improving their quality of life. Today, Edna also serves as a Parent Leader, guiding other families to access support and rebuild their lives.
